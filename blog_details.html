Chasing Light in the Dolomites

Published: June 2025

The Dolomites are a photographer’s dream — rugged peaks that glow pink at dawn, deep valleys that breathe mist into sunrise skies, and winding roads that lead to moments of unexpected beauty. In this journal entry, I recount a 3-day journey through this stunning alpine region, focused solely on chasing light. From pre-dawn hikes to sunlit ridgelines, every frame captured was a product of patience, cold fingers, and unfiltered wonder.

On the first morning, I reached Seceda an hour before sunrise. It was quiet — no tourists, just the faint rustle of wind and anticipation. As the sun began to break the horizon, the famous ridgeline lit up like a stage. Golden rays painted long shadows, and I captured what would become my favorite photo of the trip.

Over the next two days, I chased that same feeling — a light that transformed landscapes into emotion. I photographed beneath thunderclouds and beside still lakes, watching as reflections turned mountains upside down. Photography here wasn’t about perfection. It was about presence.

I returned home not just with a full memory card, but with a renewed sense of why I do what I do: to witness, to wait, and to share the quiet wonder that still exists in this world.
